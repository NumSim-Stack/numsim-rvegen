## When you need the commercial licence

You need a commercial licence if **any** of the following apply:

- You ship a closed-source product that links rvegen (statically or
  dynamically) and you do not want to release that product under GPL-3.0.
- You modify rvegen and want to keep your modifications proprietary.
- Your organisation's policy prohibits GPL-licensed dependencies in
  production code.
- You want indemnification, written warranty, or commercial support.

## When the GPL-3.0 is sufficient

You can use rvegen under GPL-3.0 alone if:

- Your project is itself open-source under a GPL-compatible licence
  (GPL-2.0+, GPL-3.0, AGPL-3.0).
- You're using rvegen for internal research that you don't redistribute.
- You're using rvegen in academic work and your contribution back is
  the publication itself.

If you're unsure, the safe assumption for a corporate or proprietary
product is that you need the commercial licence.

## FAQ

**Can I evaluate rvegen under GPL-3.0 first and switch to commercial later?**
Yes. Evaluation under GPL is fine; convert to commercial before you
distribute or deploy non-GPL derivative work.

**Do my own contributions to the open-source rvegen repository need to be
GPL-3.0?**
Yes — contributions to the public repository are accepted under GPL-3.0
only. The commercial licence applies to *use*, not to upstream
contributions.

**Does the commercial licence cover GeometricTools and other vendored
dependencies?**
The vendored GeometricTools headers under `external/GeometricTools/`
carry their own licence (Boost Software License). The commercial rvegen
licence does not modify or override those terms — you remain bound by
the upstream licence of each vendored component.
